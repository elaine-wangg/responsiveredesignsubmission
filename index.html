Identifying Usability Problems

Finding Problems

Efficiency: Can typical tasks be done quickly?

Purposes of Webpage

  • Identifying services (found in the links below its introduction).
  • Enrolling in insurance and current care (found in the links below its introduction).
  • Locations and hours / contact information (found in the header bar).
  • Community and careers (found in the header bar).
  • Paying medical bills online (found in the header bar).

Overall, typical tasks can be done quickly within 3-4 clicks since most of the links are self-explanatory. However, the layout may make it difficult to view due to font size.

Learnability: Is it easy to learn?

Layout of Webpage

  • Small headings bar containing information about page navigation and current page is underlined.
  • Central blue block of information containing information about Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQCHs) and a number to call for language assistance services.
  • Below is a set of common links with relevant news articles for credibility. However, text flows beyond the box.

Overall, the page is simple with important information located at the very top of the page and below its introduction.

Memorability: Is it easy to recall what to do?

In general, the website's usability for recall is limited. The information is distributed throughout the text and images, making it challenging to remember the location of specific information or links without thorough scanning. However, users typically expect a navigation bar on most pages, which helps them understand where to find information.

WebAIM WAVE

The WebAIM WAVE tool has identified issues with the language of the document not being identified or having an invalid lang attribute value. I largely agree with this assessment. However, upon initial page load, an audio recording provides a phone number, which aids visually impaired users in accessing website information via phone. Additionally, the tool has flagged missing alternative text for images, which means that screen reader users or those with images disabled may not have access to image content. Moreover, without regions and ARIA landmarks to identify significant page areas, users may find it difficult to navigate through the content efficiently. They may struggle to understand the overall structure of the page and find specific sections quickly.
